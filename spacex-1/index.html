SpaceX Dragon

First Private Spacecraft to the Space Station

Dragon Vehicle Overview

Dragon is a free-flying spacecraft designed to deliver both cargo and people to orbiting destinations. Dragon made history in 2012 when it became the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to the International Space Station and safely return cargo to Earth. It is the only spacecraft currently flying that is capable of returning significant amounts of cargo to Earth.

Dragon was designed from the beginning to carry humans. Under an agreement with NASA, SpaceX is now developing the refinements that will enable Dragon to fly crew. Dragon's first manned test flight is expected to take place as early as 2018.

Configurations

Cargo

Resupply the Space Station

  • Delivery and Return Services: Dragon is the first commercial spacecraft to deliver cargo to the ISS and the only cargo spacecraft capable of returning cargo to Earth.
  • Versatile Cargo Racks: The racks are designed for efficient packing in a zero-gravity environment and can accommodate various NASA cargo bags, including freezers for biological samples.

Crew

Taking Humans into Space

  • Partnership with NASA: Dragon was designed to carry humans, with upgrades in progress to support crew missions.
  • Crew Upgrades: Dragon will have seating for seven, life-support systems, manual controls, and a powered launch escape system, making it the world’s safest crew transport vehicle.
  • First Launch: Dragon’s first manned test flight is expected to take place as early as 2018.

Dragon Lab

A Free-Flying Microgravity Laboratory

  • Facilitating Research: DragonLab is an unmanned platform for research and testing in a microgravity environment.
  • Longevity: It can transport experiments and sensors into space for durations from one week to two years.
  • Capabilities: DragonLab supports space physics, radiation effects, relativity experiments, and other microgravity tests.
