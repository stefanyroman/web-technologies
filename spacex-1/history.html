SpaceX Dragon - History

From development to success, explore the history of the SpaceX Dragon spacecraft.

Dragon Spacecraft Development

SpaceX began developing the Dragon spacecraft in late 2004, with a public announcement made in 2006 and a plan for entering service in 2009. In 2006, SpaceX also won a contract to use Dragon for NASA's Commercial Resupply Services to the International Space Station (ISS).

During its maiden flight in December 2010, Dragon became the first commercially built and operated spacecraft to be recovered successfully from orbit. On May 25, 2012, a cargo variant of Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to successfully rendezvous with and attach to the ISS.

Dragon's reusable nature was a key factor in increasing NASA's partnerships with the domestic commercial aviation and aeronautics industry.
